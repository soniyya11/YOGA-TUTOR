MAKARASANA

Makarasana Yoga Pose

Makarasana or “Crocodile Pose” is a deeply restorative pose that helps relax your nervous system and strengthens the muscles in your back. This beginner yoga pose is often used to alleviate the pain that may be experienced due to practising other yoga poses. It is often performed as a concluding pose to end a yoga session.

How to do Makarasana Yoga Pose

  1. Step-1: Lie down on the floor on your stomach with your hands folded under the head.
  2. Step-2: Place the right palm over the left palm on the ground and place the head over the right palm in a relaxed way and close your eyes.
  3. Step-3: Stretch the legs as far as possible. The toes should point outwards.
  4. Step-4: Relax the whole body. Breathe normally and slowly. Feel the whole body touching the ground and the deep relaxation in all your muscles.
  5. Step-5: Relax in this posture for 2-5 minutes.
  6. Step-6: While returning from the posture, slowly bring the feet together. Unfold the arms and come to the Prone Position.

Benefits of Makarasana Yoga Pose

  • 1)It reduces physical and mental stress.
  • 2)Ensures health of the lungs.
  • 3)Beneficial in problems such as slip disk and sciatica.
  • 4)Provides relief in asthma and respiratory problems.
  • 5)Provides strength and flexibility to the spine.
  • 6)Effective in keeping high blood pressure under control.
  • 7)Ensure proper working of the small intestine and strengthens the digestive system.

Precautions of Makarasana Yoga Pose

  • 1. Pregnant women are prohibited from doing this asana.
  • 2.People with heart problems and excessive obesity should not perform makarasana.
  • 3.Ensure that your elbows are comfortable when doing this asana.
  • 4.Increase the duration of the exercise gradually according to your comfort.
