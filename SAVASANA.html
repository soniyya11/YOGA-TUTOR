SAVASANA

Savasana Yoga Pose

Savasana is also known as Corpse Pose. The name comes from the Sanskrit words "shava" (corpse) and "asana" (pose). The pose is often used at the end of a yoga session to promote relaxation and integration.

How to do Savasana Yoga Pose

  1. 1.Lie down on your back. Stretch your arms and legs out and away from your body. Keep your eyes gently closed.
  2. 2.Breathe evenly and attempt to relax. Focus on your breath and let your body melt into the floor.
  3. 3.Scan your body from head to toe and release any tension you may be holding. Pay attention to your toes, feet, ankles, calves, thighs and every part.
  4. 4.To come out of Savasana, gently wiggle your fingers and toes. Slowly open your eyes and take a few deep breaths.

    5. Additional tips

  5. 1.Use a yoga mat or a blanket to lie on. This will help you to feel more comfortable and supported.
  6. 2.Wear comfortable clothing. You don't want to be restricted in any way.
  7. 3.Find a quiet place to practice. This will help you to focus on your breath and relaxation.
  8. 4.If you find it difficult to relax your mind, try to focus on a mantra or a visualization.

Benefits of Savasana Yoga Pose

  • 1.Reduces stress and anxiety:
  • Savasana helps to activate the parasympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for relaxation. This can help to reduce stress, anxiety, and tension in the body.
  • 2.Improves sleep :
  • Savasana can help to improve sleep quality by promoting relaxation and reducing stress. It can also help to regulate the body's natural sleep-wake cycle.
  • 3.Stay in Savasana for at least 5-10 minutes:
  • You can stay for longer if you like.
  • 4.can your body from head to toe and release any tension you may be holding:
  • Pay attention to your toes, feet, ankles, calves, thighs, hips, stomach, back, chest, shoulders, arms, hands, neck, face, and head.

Precautions while doing Sukhasana Yoga Pose

  • May cause discomfort for people with certain physical conditions:
  • For example, people with lower back pain or sciatica may find Savasana to be uncomfortable or even painful.
  • May be difficult for people with restless minds or anxiety:
  • The stillness and focus required for Savasana can be challenging for people who have difficulty calming their minds or who experience anxiety.
  • May not be appropriate for people who are pregnant or breastfeeding.
  • Pregnant women should consult with their doctor before practicing Savasana, and breastfeeding women may find that their breasts become engorged in the pose.
