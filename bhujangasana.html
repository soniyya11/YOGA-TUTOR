BHUJANGASANA

Bhujangasana Yoga Pose

Bhujangasana, also known as Cobra Pose, is a versatile yoga pose that offers a wide range of benefits for both the physical and mental body. It is a backbending pose that strengthens the spine, opens up the chest, and improves flexibility. Additionally, Bhujangasana stimulates the heart and lungs, enhances digestion.

How to do Bhujangasana Yoga Pose

  1. Step 1: Lie on your stomach
  2. Lie on your stomach with your legs extended and your toes pointed. Place your palms flat on the floor under your shoulders, with your fingertips pointing forward. Relax your shoulders and keep your elbows close to your body.
  3. Step 2: Inhale and lift your chest.
  4. As you inhale, slowly press your palms into the floor and lift your chest off the mat. Keep your hips pressed down into the mat. Straighten your arms as much as you can without straining your shoulders.
  5. Step 3: Lengthen your spine
  6. As you hold the pose, lengthen your spine and keep your neck in a neutral position. Look straight ahead or slightly down at your mat. Breathe deeply and evenly.
  7. Step 4: Hold for 5-10 breaths
  8. Hold the pose for 5-10 breaths, or as long as you can comfortably.
  9. Step 5: Release the pose
  10. To release the pose, exhale slowly and lower your chest back down to the mat. Rest in Child's Pose for a few breaths before coming to a seated position.

Benefits of Bhujangasana Yoga Pose

  • 1. Strengthens the spine:
  • Bhujangasana engages the muscles along the spine, particularly the erector spinae, which provide support and stability to the vertebral column.
  • 2.Increases spinal flexibility:
  • The gentle backbending action of Bhujangasana stretches and lengthens the muscles surrounding the spine, improving overall flexibility and range of motion.
  • 3.Enhances chest expansion:
  • By opening up the chest, Bhujangasana allows for deeper breathing and improved lung function. It also stretches the intercostal muscles, which are responsible for expanding the rib cage during inhalation.
  • 4.Tones the abdominal muscles:
  • Bhujangasana engages the core muscles, including the transverse abdominis, rectus abdominis, and obliques, promoting muscle tone and stability in the abdominal region.

Precautions while doing Bhujangasana Yoga Pose

  • 1.Back pain:
  • Bhujangasana is a backbending pose, and it can put strain on the spine if it is not performed properly. People with back pain, especially those with herniated discs or other spinal conditions, should avoid or modify the pose.
  • 2.Wrist pain:
  • Bhujangasana requires the use of the wrists to support the weight of the body. People with wrist pain or injuries should modify the pose by keeping the elbows bent and the palms flat on the floor.
  • .3.Neck pain:
  • The neck can be strained if it is not kept in a neutral position during Bhujangasana. People with neck pain or injuries should keep the chin tucked in and avoid looking up.
