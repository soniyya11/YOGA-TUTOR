KAPOTASANA

Kapotasana Yoga Pose

Kapotasana is an strong hip opener that improves flexibility throughout the body. From Sanskrit, kapota means “pigeon” and asana means “pose” or “posture.”This asana is a half split with one leg stretched back so the top of the foot presses into the mat and the other leg is bent in front with the calf resting on the ground. The torso lifts with a slight arch in the back.

How to do Kapotasana Yoga Pose

  • Step-1: First put down your Knee on the floor and your spine should be erect.
  • Step-2:Now gently stretch your right leg in backward direction (behind you).
  • Step-3:Keep extending your right leg until your left knee and foot are adjacent to the right hip.
  • Step-4:Keep stretching your right leg until your left knee and foot are adjoining the right hip.
  • Step-5:Make sure that your toes ought to be pointed.
  • Step-6:After that you have to lean forward and breathe in deeply.
  • Step-7:Now push your mid – section (chest) outwards.
  • Step-8:Remain in the position about 20-25 seconds.
  • Step-9:Now switch (change) your leg and repeat the same process with your opposite leg.
  • Step-10:After that sit in Padmasana and rest for while and repeat the process 4 times with your both legs.

Benefits of Kapotasana Yoga Pose

  • 1.Pigeon Pose stretches and strengthens the joints and muscles in your legs.
  • 2.Helps to reduce stiffness in the back and hips.
  • 3.Kapotasana balances the blood pressure and also reduces the effects of chronic diseases.
  • 4.Releases stress and calms the mind and the body.

Precautions While doing kapotasana Yoga Pose

  • Injury to the spine, the neck or the shoulders.
  • Insomnia
  • Stiff back or a sore back.
  • Migraine
  • Hypertension
  • Weak functions of any organ.
  • Women who are pregnant or menstruating.
  • Spondylitis
  • Any kind of allergies related to the breathing.
  • Depression
