CHAKRASANA

Chakrasana Yoga Pose

Chakrasana or wheel pose is a full body asana where one curves the spine to do a backward bend. It is called wheel pose, as the final posture of the body resembles a round wheel. It provides a good exercise for the back and the chest and stomach muscles.

How to do Chakrasana Yoga Pose

  1. Step-1:Stand straight with your feet slightly apart.
  2. Step-2:Place your palms against your lower back and push your pelvic region slightly forward.
  3. Step-3:Exhale. Curve your trunk backwards. Support the weight of your body with your thighs.
  4. Step-4:Slowly stretch your arms over your head as you continue to bend backwards.
  5. Step-5:Bend till your palms drop to the floor behind you.
  6. Step-6:Immediately straighten your palms to support your body and to avoid a fall.
  7. Step-7:Your body should form an arch.
  8. Step-8:Breathe evenly. Maintain your position for a few seconds.

Precautions While doing Chakrasana Yoga Pose

  • Do not perform Chakrasana directly by yourself, it can injure your back, before doing this pose try some backbend yoga poses and surya namskar also of hatha yoga.
  • Chakrasana is the advanced level yoga pose. So if you are not comfortable and ready and if you have pain in your back, so don’t practice.
  • People who are suffering from headaches and high blood pressure problems so should avoid this pose.
  • The women who are pregnant don’t do this asana, you should avoid this until your delivery.
  • The practice of wheel pose is according to your body’s needs, do not force your body. Doing forcefully of this pose you can get injured in your lower back and shoulder and wrist.
