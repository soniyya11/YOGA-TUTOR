Dhanurasana Yoga Pose

Spreading the legs on the ground, straight like a stick, and catching hold of the feet with the hands, and making the body bent like a bow, is called by the Yogis the Dhanurasana or Bow-posture. Dhanurasana is used in the classical Indian dance form Bharatanatyam.

How to do Dhanurasana Yoga Pose

  1. Step-1: Lie down flat on your abdomen.
  2. Step-1:Ensure that your forehead is placed against the floor and that your legs are close together.
  3. Step-1:Bend your legs at the knees and bring your feet up behind you.
