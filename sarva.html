SARVANGASANA

Sarvangasana Yoga Pose

Sarvangasana is a posture where the whole body is balanced on the shoulder and is therefore, also known as a Shoulder stand. Sarvangasana constitute 3 words “Sarva”, “anga” and “asana”. “Sarva” means “all”, “anga” means body part and “asana” means posture.

How to do Sarvangasana Yoga Pose

  1. Step-1: Lie down flat on your back. Keep your legs close together and your chin tucked in.
  2. Step-2:Breathe deeply. Inhale. Lift your legs upwards till your feet are pointing directly up - that is, till your legs are perpendicular to your torso.
  3. Step-3:Tuck your hands underneath yourself with your palms facing up. With your hands under your buttocks, prepare to raise your torso.
  4. Step-4:Lift your torso gradually till you are resting on your shoulders.
  5. Step-5:Your arms must now be bent at the elbow. Bring your hands to your back to support yourself. Your legs should be over your head.
  6. Step-6:Slowly straighten your back.
  7. Step-7:Keep your knees firm but relax your calf muscles now. Maintain the posture.
  8. Step-8:To relax, lower your legs till your feet are at an angle of 45 degreesover your head. Lower your arms to the floor. Unwind gradually.

Precautions While doing Sarvangasana Yoga Pose

  • While performing Sarvangasana, it is recommended not to bend your knees.
  • Don’t try to overstrain your neck or move it.
  • Your legs should be relaxed, don’t tense it as this will contract your legs and prevents the drainage of the blood.
  • It is advisable to not bring your legs over the head while performing this yoga poses if it causes tension.
