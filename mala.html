MALASANA

Malasana Yoga Pose

The most common translation of Malasana is Garland pose (some also refer to it as squat pose). Its symbolism is tightly related to the importance of accepting the cyclical nature of life. A “mala” is a string of 108 beads used for chanting mantras and meditation.

How to do Malasana Yoga Pose

  • Step-1:Stand at the front of your yoga mat with your feet a bit wider than your hips.
  • Step-2:Turn your toes out about 45 degrees.
  • Step-3:Bend your knees and lower your butt as close to the floor as possible.
  • Step-4:Once you are in a position you can sustain, let your butt sink toward the floor and lift your chest.
  • Step-5:Take your elbows to the insides of your knees. Bring your hands to Anjali Mudra at your heart. You can apply a little pressure with your elbows to widen the knees.
  • Step-6:Stay here for five to ten cycles of breath.
  • Step-7:Straighten your legs to come out.

Benefits of Malasana Yoga Pose

  • 1.Opens your hips and groin
  • 2.Stretches your ankles, lower hamstrings, back and neck
  • 3.Tones your abdominals
  • 4.Aids in digestion
  • 5.Strengthens your metabolism
  • 6.Keeps your pelvic and hip joints healthy
  • 7.Ideal for prenatal yoga

Precautions While doing Malasana Yoga Pose

  • Try practising this asana on an empty stomach.
  • Divide the whole-body weight evenly throughout the whole feet.
  • Do not overdo this asana in a single session.
  • Don't push yourself into a deeper squat than your body allows you to.
  • Avoid doing this asana if you have knee pain.
  • Do not perform Malasana if you have suffered injuries on your back, hips, knees, ankles, or feet.
